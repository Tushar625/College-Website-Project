Political Science (B.A Hons)

Intrigued by world politics? Inspired by the work of political analysts? Our three-year Honours course in Political Science, which covers a range of topics from comparative politics and government to political sociology and international relations, gives you the tools for a career in diverse fields like public administration, rural development and international aid agencies to publishing and journalism.

Programme Specific Outcomes (PSO)

PSO1: Understanding the basic premises of political theories and institutions.

PSO2: Analyze the relations between political functionaries, thinkers, ideas and narratives to create linkages with contemporary society.

PSO3: Understand the socio-political and cultural development of society with intra- national and international systems at work

PSO4: Acquisition of the ability to critically perceive the historical timeline of political events both by means of formal and informal relationships.

PSO5: Imparting sound knowledge about the political situation and the effects of identities and institutions on the system.

Course Eligibility

A candidate taking up Honours Course in a subject must have obtained at the previous qualifying examination a minimum of:

55% marks in Political Science

OR

45% marks in Political Science + 50% marks in BO4

Note:

If the candidate has not studied Political Science in class XII, he / she needs to achieve the above-mentioned marks in English to apply for Political Science (H).
